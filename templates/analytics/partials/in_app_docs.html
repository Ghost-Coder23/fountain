Help & Documentation (How to Use the System)

This guide explains where to find key features and how to complete common tasks across the modules.

1) Analytics Dashboards (Role-based)

When you log in, the Analytics dashboard you see depends on your role.

  • Admin / Headmaster: School-level dashboards, finance summary tiles, attendance status, and shortcuts to fee management.
  • Senior / Secretary: Operational workflows (invoices list + payments) and attendance/result progress.
  • Teacher: Assigned subject/classwork view and pending result entry status.
  • Parent / Student: Child/student performance, attendance summary, and fee balance.
2) Schools (roles & access)

Use the school dashboards to manage daily operations under your assigned role.

  • Headmaster/Admin: Access approvals and fee management actions shown on the dashboard.
  • Teacher: Enter results for assigned classes/subjects when permitted by the current term workflow.
3) Academics (students, classes, subjects)
  • Students: Enroll students from the dashboard shortcuts (e.g. Enroll Student).
  • Classes/Sections: Create/manage class sections used for enrollment and results entry.
  • Subjects & assignments: Teachers are linked to subjects and class sections so results entry knows where to save marks.

Tip: Many finance pages reference students/class sections—keep academics data up to date.

4) Results (CA + Exam + approval)

Results follow a status workflow. Use this as your checklist when marks are not showing where expected.

  • Teacher: Enters marks for assigned subject/class/term.
  • Submitted: Teacher completed entry; results are waiting for approval.
  • Approved / Locked: Headmaster approved and the term is finalized for that result set (updates/edits restricted).
5) Attendance (sessions & recording)
  • Teacher/Admin: Mark attendance for each class session.
  • Finalized: A session can be marked finalized (used by dashboards to calculate completion).
  • Dashboards: Attendance widgets show completion for today and weekly trend.
6) Fees & Invoices (core finance workflow)

This section covers fee structures, invoice generation, payments, statuses, and related finance actions.

6.1 View invoices and statuses
  • Invoice list: Open invoices from the dashboard shortcuts (e.g. Record Payment / invoice list pages).
  • Search/filter: Use the search box to filter invoices by student/account details, then open a specific invoice using View.
  • Status meaning:
    • Unpaid: No confirmed payment applied yet.
    • Partial: Some confirmed payment applied; balance remains.
    • Overdue: Due date is in the past and still unpaid/partial.
6.2 Generate monthly invoices (manual + automatic)
  • Manual run: Go to Payment Settings and use Run now to generate invoices immediately.
  • Automatic generation: Enable Auto-generate Invoices and set the day to automate monthly creation.
  • Automation requirement: Automatic invoice generation depends on a server-side cron job.
6.3 Record payments
  • Where to record: Use the Finance Dashboard or open an invoice detail page/modal and record the payment.
  • Confirmation: When payment is saved as confirmed, invoice balances update.
  • Overpayment handling: If payment exceeds invoice balance, the extra can be moved to Student Credit.
6.4 Student fee statement
  • Open statement: From a student profile or fee section, open Student Fee Statement to view invoices and payment summary.
6.5 Expenses (school spending)
  • Record expenses: Use the Expense section to add expense categories and expense entries.
  • Analytics: Dashboards may use revenue vs expense trends.
7) Automation (cron) — Monthly invoice generation

Automatic monthly invoice generation requires a cron job that runs once per day.

  • Ensure the cron job calls the correct invoice generation management command.
  • Pass the correct --school argument (or schedule logic) so invoices are generated for the intended school tenant.
  • If you do not want automation, keep Auto-generate Invoices disabled and use Run now for manual runs.
