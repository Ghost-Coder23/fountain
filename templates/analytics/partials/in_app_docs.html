Help & Documentation (How to Use the System)
Quick Help — Finance & Invoices

This short guide explains the key finance features available from the dashboard.

    • View invoices: Click any finance tile (Unpaid, Partial, Overdue) to open the invoice modal. Use the search box to filter and click "View" to see invoice details.
    • Run monthly invoice generation: Go to Payment Settings and use the "Run now" button to generate invoices immediately. Enable Auto-generate Invoices and set the day to automate monthly generation (requires a cron job as described below).
    • Student details: Open a student profile to see their invoices and payment summary.
    • Recording payments: Use the Finance Dashboard or the invoice detail page to record payments. Confirmed payments update invoice balances automatically.
    2) Schools (roles & access)

    Use the school dashboards to manage daily operations under your assigned role.

  • Headmaster/Admin: Can access approvals and fee management actions shown on the dashboard.
  • Teacher: Can enter results for assigned classes/subjects (when the term is open/allowed).
    • 3) Academics (students, classes, subjects)
    • Students: Add/enroll students via the dashboard shortcuts (e.g. Enroll Student).
    • Classes/Sections: Create/manage class sections used for enrollment and results entry.
    • Subjects & Assignments: Teachers are linked to subjects and class sections so results entry knows where to save marks.

    Tip: Many finance pages reference students and class sections, so keep academics data up to date.

    4) Results (CA + Exam + approval)

    Results follow a status workflow. Use this as your checklist when marks are not showing where expected.

    • Teacher: Enters marks for assigned subject/class/term.
    • Submitted: Teacher completed entry and the result is waiting for approval.
    • Approved / Locked: Headmaster approved and the term is effectively finalized for that result set (updates and edits are restricted).
    5) Attendance (sessions & recording)
    • Teacher/Admin: Mark attendance for each class session.
    • Finalized: A session can be marked as finalized (used by dashboards to calculate completion).
    • Dashboards: Attendance widgets show completion for today and the weekly trend.
    6) Fees & Invoices (core finance workflow)

    This section covers fee structures, invoice generation, payments, and invoice statuses.

    6.1 View invoices and statuses
    • Invoice list: Open the invoice list from the dashboard shortcut (e.g. Record Payment or Invoices).
    • Search/filter: Use the search box on the dashboard to filter students/invoices, then use View to open the invoice detail modal/page.
    • Status meaning:
      • Unpaid: No confirmed payment applied yet.
      • Partial: Some confirmed payment applied, but balance remains.
      • Overdue: Invoice due date is in the past and still unpaid/partial.
    6.2 Generate monthly invoices (manual + automatic)
    • Manual run: Go to Payment Settings and use Run now to generate invoices immediately.
    • Automatic generation: Enable Auto-generate Invoices and set the day to automate monthly creation.
    • Automation requirement: Automatic invoice generation depends on a server-side cron job.
    6.3 Record payments
    • Where to record: Use the Finance Dashboard or open an individual invoice and record payment from the invoice detail page/modal.
    • Confirmation: Once a payment is saved as confirmed, invoice balances update accordingly.
    • Overpayment handling: If payment exceeds the invoice balance, the system can move the extra into Student Credit.
    6.4 Student fee statement
    • Open statement: From the student profile or from the fee section, open Student Fee Statement to view invoices and payment summary.
    6.5 Expenses (school spending)
    • Record expenses: Use the Expense section to add expense categories and expense entries.
    • Analytics: Finance dashboards may use revenue vs expense trends.
    7) Automation (cron) — Monthly invoice generation

    Automatic monthly invoice generation requires a cron job that runs once per day.

    • Ensure the cron job uses the correct management command for invoice generation.
    • Pass the correct --school argument (or schedule logic) so invoices are generated for the intended school tenant.
    • If you do not want automation, keep Auto-generate Invoices disabled and use Run now for manual runs.
